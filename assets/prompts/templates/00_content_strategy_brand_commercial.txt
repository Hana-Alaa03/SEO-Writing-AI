### COMMERCIAL STRATEGY ARCHITECTURE (STRICT)
- **Primary Objective**: Help the reader make a clear decision via trust and helpfulness (Bottom-of-Funnel Clarity).
- **Core Philosophy**: 70% Transactional focus / 30% Supportive Education. 
- **Tone**: Grounded, helpful, peer-to-peer, and persuasive but NOT pushy. 
- **Service-Page Standards**: Built for decision speed and scannability.

### MANDATORY SECTION MAPPING (IN ORDER):
1. **The Hook (Intro)**: Address the user's specific problem/need immediately. Primary Keyword in paragraph 1. Optional SOFT CTA at the end of the intro.
2. **The Essence (What is it?)**: H2 with Primary Keyword. Clear, concise, and professional service/product description.
3. **The Value (Key Features)**: Bullet points are preferred for high scannability. Show exactly what is included.
4. **The Edge (Brand Advantages)**: Why choose this brand over the market? Specific advantages only. Avoid generic "Quality/Experience" claims.
5. **The Proof (Trust Building)**: Stats, case studies, client results, or factual evidence. This is where you earn the conversion.
6. **The Matrix (Comparison)**: Service tiers, tiered pricing, or comparison vs market standards. Flexible but scannable format.
7. **The Path (How it Works)**: Numbered steps defining the operational process to reduce friction and eliminate uncertainty.
8. **The Support (FAQ)**: Focus on objections and pricing/process questions specifically. MAXIMUM 5 questions.
9. **The Close (Conclusion)**: Strong, direct, and confident sign-off + Final Primary CTA for conversion.
