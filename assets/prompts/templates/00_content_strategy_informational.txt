### INFORMATIONAL STRATEGY ARCHITECTURE (STRICT)
- **Primary Objective**: Educate, empower, and help the reader (Building Practical Authority).
- **Core Philosophy**: 100% Educational / 0% Direct Selling. No Promotional fluff.
- **Tone**: Helpfully neutral, conversational (Modern Standard Arabic), and advisor-like.

### MANDATORY SECTION MAPPING (IN ORDER):
1. **The Foundation (Intro)**: Hook + reader context + why the topic matters now. Primary Keyword integrated naturally. 0% Selling. NO CTA allowed. Do NOT make the intro a definition section.
2. **The Definition (What is)**: First visible H2 with Primary Keyword. Clear, authoritative, and concise description. This must be separate from the intro.
3. **The Relevance (Why it matters)**: Practical context building + Key Benefits for the reader.
4. **The Knowledge (Main Body)**: Diverse sub-topic exploration. Each H2/H3 must cover a specific step or sub-theme using LSI keywords as primary signals.
5. **The Action (Practical Tips)**: Actionable "How-to" examples or practical advice to maximize dwell time and utility.
6. **The Caution (Mistakes)**: High-value section addressing common pitfalls. Builds high trust and handles silent objections.
7. **The Clarity (FAQ)**: Minimum 4-5 questions targeting long-tail related queries in People may ask.
8. **The Summary (Conclusion)**: Summary of key takeaways + ONE soft CTA at most (e.g., "Learn more" or "Explore further").

### EXPERIENCE / DESTINATION TOPICS
If the primary keyword is a place, venue, destination, attraction, event area, mall, restaurant, or real-world visitor experience:
- The intro must create interest and orient the visitor, but the first visible H2 must still define what the place is.
- Do NOT group independent visitor needs into one broad section.
- Location/access, opening hours/timing, tickets/pricing/booking, parking/transport, and visitor tips are separate intents when relevant.
- Prefer more focused H2 sections over compressed headings. It is better to have 9-10 clean sections than 7 overloaded sections.
